What is Budgeting?

Budgeting is the way of tracking your money. People tend to create a budget plan which helps you in the long run; it helps decide if you have money to buy the things you need or want and helps it will help save for the future. Budgeting will keep you track of not being in debt or helps you get out of debt.

College students have the hardest time of managing their money because they do not have a lot of money so they might be in debt or they don’t know how manage their money. If you are one of these people, then you came to the right place. Learn more on how to manage your money.

How to start a budget plan?
  1. Track your income weekly
    • Determine how much money you get from work or other ways which includes cash gifts, support from others, selling your items that you do not need
  2. Track spending and review at end of the week
    • It is good to use 30% of your income on wants and 50% of your income on needs
  3. Start saving a certain percentage of your income
    • It is good to put money on the side for saving because if there is an emergency you can use money from the savings instead of cutting your expenses
    • It is good to save at least 20% of your income to savings
  4. Record spending/income/saving and track progress weekly
    • Track your information on a spreadsheet or on a notebook
  5. Analyze what you recorded
    • If you are spending more than your income or you want to save more, then you need to cut out some expensive

Tips on Cutting Expenses:

  • Shopping
  • Eating Out
  • Entertainment
  • Groceries
  • Transportaion

Shopping


  • Find as many coupons as possible, look to magazines and online for them

  • Buy used items that are in good condition instead of buying new items at full price

  • Buy items when they are in sale

  • Shop only when you need to

Eating out


  • Limit eating out to once or twice a week

  • Find discounts or special deals at restaurants

  • Eat at cheap places for example, McDonalds or Burger King

Entertainment


  • Look for free or discounted events like concerts or festivals near by

  • Do stuff at home like playing board gamesto entertain you and your friends

  • Cancel gym or any other memberships that you do not use anymore

Groceries


  • Use as much coupons as possible

  • Buy generic brands instead of name brands(Click here for an example)

  • Do not buy more than you need

Transportaion


  • Buy a cheap bike and use it to get to places

  • Use public transportation

  • Walk, it is free!
